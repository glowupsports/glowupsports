Side-by-side comparison tables for junior belt levels
These tables compare what each body makes publicly available. For non-USTA bodies, you’ll see: sublevels are Unspecified (publicly), but stage-level environment + scoring/format are often available.

Red 3 → Red 2 → Red 1
Level	USTA (published competencies)	Tennis10s / International standard	Tennis Australia	Tennis Canada	LTA	KNLTB Tenniskids
Red 3	Shots/technique: drop-hits to target; simple low‑to‑high swing; volley block with racquet head above wrist. Movement: multi-direction movement, recover to center; square stance basics; shoulder turn. Serve/return: underhand or overhand serve into correct box from inside service line; direct return back to server. Game: score with visual aid; call in/out; positions for serve/return. 
Unspecified (publicly) as Red 3/2/1. Stage-level: Red (often 5–8) uses ~11–12m × 5–6m court, 80cm net; slower balls; short formats like tiebreaks to 7/10, short sets to 4, timed matches. 
Unspecified (publicly) as Red 3/2/1. Stage-level technique fundamentals (Red 5–8): athletic ready position; Eastern→semi‑western FH; contact in front; circular swing; low‑to‑high path; step forward; basic block volleys; serve with basic throwing action and toss release around eye level. 
Unspecified (publicly) as Red 3/2/1. Curriculum emphasizes point-play and “Road to Rally” progressions across Red/Orange/Green/Yellow. 
Unspecified (publicly) as Red 3/2/1. Publishes Red court equipment specs (11m × 5.5m; 80cm net; 17–23" racquet). 
Unspecified (publicly) as Red 3/2/1. Publishes Red match rules: first to 7 points; simple counting; alternating serve; overhand/underhand allowed; two attempts. 
Red 2	Shots/technique: rally over net from service line; unit turn; trunk rotation; approach + volley from feed; volley deep/short; developing 1HBH volley. Serve/return: overhand serve to FH/BH; return 2nd serve deep. Footwork: split-step before volley; diagonal movement to volley; recover to center when outside court. Game: announce score; win by one/two; shake hands.

Red 1	Shots/technique: baseline rally crosscourt/DTL/side-to-side; developing topspin; slice FH/BH from feed; hit to open space; learning to take deep ball on the rise. Footwork/stances: open/semi-open stance on wide/deep balls; crossover recovery; step out for unit turn. Serve: continuous service motion; continental grip; developing slice/body serve; coordinate knee bend with toss. Transition: approach to one side then volley open court; overhead to center; establish trophy position for overhead prep. Game: faults/lets; can play best-of-three tiebreaks.

Orange 3 → Orange 2 → Orange 1
Level	USTA (published competencies)	Tennis10s / International standard	Tennis Australia	Tennis Canada	LTA	KNLTB Tenniskids
Orange 3	Rally: depth to center with increased net height; can rally slice FH/BH from inside service box. Stances/footwork: square stance in rally, developing open stance on wide balls; crossover recovery; diagonal movement for approach/volley; split-step forward/diagonal for volley. Serve/return: serves consistently in box both sides; return to center with depth; moves up for 2nd serve. Transition: volley-to-groundstroke rally; approach+volley (racquet feed); overheads; basic doubles positions. 
Unspecified (publicly) as Orange 3/2/1. Stage-level: Orange (often 8–10) uses 18m × 6.5–8.23m court; net 80–91cm; scoring options include tiebreaks or short sets; team formats encouraged. 
Unspecified (publicly) as Orange 3/2/1. Stage-level technique: earlier prep (backswing complete by bounce); contact in front; vertical face for long hitting zone; square stance step forward introduced; overhead/serve mechanics progress; tactical fundamentals include D‑N‑O phases and “winning plays.” 
Unspecified as Orange 3/2/1; point-play structure across ball colors. 
Unspecified as Orange 3/2/1; publishes orange court specs: singles 18m × 6.5m, doubles 18m × 8.23m, net 80cm, 23–25" racquet. 
Unspecified as Orange 3/2/1; publishes orange match rules: first to 4 games, no‑ad deciding point at 40‑40; second serve may be underhand; orange competition often timed 15–20 minutes. 
Orange 2	Rally: depth + direction to targets; more consistent topspin (FH/BH) with speed; developing slice on low midcourt balls; consistent contact in front. Serve/return: increased racquet head speed on 1st serve, spin on 2nd; direct 1st serve to FH/BH; return 2nd serve FH/BH; compact backswing on rise returns. Transition: recognize short ball; hop move on square-stance approach; overhead depth; lobs/passing DTL; one up/one back doubles; recover behind baseline after defense.

Orange 1	Rally/timing: plays deep baseline shots on the rise; consistent shape/spin; changes tempo for offense/defense; earlier prep for rise balls. Serve/return: routine; serve to wide/body/T; establish serve+1 combos; attack weak 2nd serve on rise and come to net; consistent toss inside front leg; shoulder-over-shoulder up motion. Transition: drive volley on high midcourt balls; first volley behind opponent; deep volley + short angle with spin; overhead control near service line; two-at-net doubles communication.

Green 3 → Green 2 → Green 1
Level	USTA	Tennis10s / International standard	Tennis Australia	Tennis Canada	LTA	KNLTB Tenniskids
Green 3	Unspecified (publicly). USTA publishes Green 02 and 01, not a Green 03 tier. 
Unspecified (publicly) as Green 3/2/1. Stage-level: Green (often 9–10) uses full court with 25% slower (“green dot”) ball; short sets to 4 and match tiebreak patterns appear in Tennis10s guidance. 
Unspecified as Green 3/2/1; publishes stage technique (Green 9+): adaptable stances (square/semi‑open/open); low‑to‑high acceleration; stable head at contact; plus tactical fundamentals. 
Unspecified as Green 3/2/1.

Unspecified as Green 3/2/1; publishes Green match rules: 2 sets to 4 games (win by 2); tiebreak at 4–4 to 7; deciding tiebreak if 1–1 sets; competitive day details. 
Green 2	Green 02: depth + direction; developing spin/shape; inside-out FH; topspin angle off wide midcourt balls. Footwork: wider lower base on split-step; diagonal forward/back movement and arrive balanced; stances appropriate to deep/wide/short balls. Serve/return: direct 1st serve to FH/BH; return 1st serve deep down center; step in for 2nd serve return to open court; increased leg drive; adjust toss to change spin; tossing arm ahead of hitting arm. Transition: overhead from service line; passing shots DTL/angle/lob; drop-step + crossover for deeper overheads; trophy position. Doubles: close diagonally and volley to space.

Green 1	Green 01: combines height/spin/speed to keep opponent deep; forehand dictates from different court areas; drop shots inside baseline.
